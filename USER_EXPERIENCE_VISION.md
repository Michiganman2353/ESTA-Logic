## Emotional Design Principles

### 1. **Trust Before Transaction**

Users must feel safe before they feel capable.

**Design Applications:**

- Security indicators visible throughout
- Encryption badges on document upload
- Legal accuracy statements on calculations
- Third-party audit certifications displayed
- Clear privacy policy and data handling

**Language Examples:**

- âœ… _"Your data is encrypted with bank-level security"_
- âœ… _"This calculation follows Michigan ESTA law exactly"_
- âœ… _"Your privacy is legally protected"_
- âŒ _"Upload your documents"_ (without security context)

---

### 2. **Clarity Reduces Anxiety**

Confusion creates stress. Simplicity creates confidence.

**Design Applications:**

- One question at a time in guided flows
- Progress indicators on every multi-step process
- Plain language with 8th-grade reading level
- Jargon-free explanations with optional deep-dives
- Visual feedback for every action

**Language Examples:**

- âœ… _"You have 15.5 hours available"_ (simple, direct)
- âœ… _"Here's what happens next"_ (removes uncertainty)
- âŒ _"Aggregate PTO accrual per regulatory framework"_ (jargon)

---

### 3. **Guidance Prevents Abandonment**

Users who feel supported complete journeys. Users who feel alone abandon them.

**Design Applications:**

- Contextual help at every decision point
- "What's next" messaging throughout
- Clear primary action buttons
- Optional assistance without forcing it
- Save-and-resume functionality

**Language Examples:**

- âœ… _"Let's set up your first employee together"_
- âœ… _"Need help? Here's what this means..."_
- âœ… _"You're doing great â€” almost done!"_
- âŒ Silent forms with no encouragement

### 4. **Transparency Builds Trust**

Hidden processes create suspicion. Visible processes create confidence.

**Design Applications:**

- Show calculation breakdowns on demand
- Audit trails accessible to users
- Legal references linked and explained
- Confidence scores displayed with reasoning
- "How we got this number" explanations

**Language Examples:**

- âœ… _"Here's exactly how we calculated this: ..."_
- âœ… _"This decision is based on Michigan law Section 3(a)"_
- âœ… _"We're 98% confident in this calculation because..."_
- âŒ Just showing numbers without explanation

### 5. **Reassurance Is an Active Feature**

Users need more than absence of errors â€” they need confirmation of correctness.

**Design Applications:**

## Design Language & Tone

### Voice Characteristics

**Professional, Not Corporate**

- Warm and approachable
- Confident without arrogance
- Helpful without condescension
- Clear without being simplistic

**Reassuring, Not Patronizing**

- Acknowledge stress: _"We know compliance can feel overwhelming"_
- Offer support: _"We're here to make this simple"_
- Build confidence: _"You've got this â€” we'll help"_

- Short sentences (under 20 words)
